## **How It Works — Google Sheets Power, Portal Security**

### **1. Attendance Management: From Phone To Sheet, Instantly**

**Old way:** Teacher opens Google Sheet on phone. Pinch-zoom. Tries to tap cell D47. Misclicks. Saves wrong data. Sheet lags with 2000 rows.

**Scholar MS way:**
1. Teacher opens Scholar MS app, taps “Mark Attendance”
2. Sees only their class list. Taps Present/Absent. Done in 30 seconds.
3. <span class="text-[#1DA9CE] font-semibold">Data writes directly to your Google Sheet backend.</span> No delay, no misclicks, no formula risk.
4. Principal’s dashboard updates live. Parent gets WhatsApp “Your child was marked absent today.”

Teacher never sees the Sheet. Sheet never breaks. But all data lives in Google Sheets you own.

### **2. Parent Communication: WhatsApp + Email, Auto-Triggered From Sheets**

**The problem:** 
School keeps parent numbers in a Sheet. Admin exports to WhatsApp, sends notices manually. Fee due? Clerk checks Sheet, calls parents one by one.

**Scholar MS way:**
1. Parent contacts already in your Google Sheet
2. Fee due date column hits “tomorrow” → <span class="text-[#1DA9CE] font-semibold">System auto-sends WhatsApp + Email from Scholar MS</span>
3. Student absent → Auto WhatsApp to parent in their language
4. Report card generated in Sheet → Auto PDF + email to parent

No exporting CSVs. No manual messaging. Google Sheet triggers it, Scholar MS delivers it.

### **3. Efficient Fee Management: Sheet As Ledger, Portal As Interface**

**The problem:** 
Clerk opens Fee Sheet. Sorts, filters, adds row. Forgets to update formula for total. Balance wrong. Parent disputes.

**Scholar MS way:**
1. Clerk opens “Collect Fee” portal, searches student, enters amount
2. <span class="text-[#1DA9CE] font-semibold">System writes to Google Sheet Fee tab using locked formulas you set.</span> Total, balance, late fee — all auto-calculate.
3. Receipt PDF generated. SMS sent. Defaulter list auto-updates in Sheet.
4. Principal opens dashboard → sees live collection vs target, pulled from Sheet.

Clerk cannot break formulas. Cannot delete rows. Cannot “forget” to update total. Sheet stays clean because only Scholar MS writes to it.

### **4. Teacher Management: Roles, Workload, Payroll — All From Sheets**

Teacher details, subjects, classes, salaries live in your Google Sheet “Staff” tab.

Scholar MS reads it and gives you:

1. **Staff Portal** — Teacher logs in, sees only their timetable, their students, their tasks
2. **Workload Dashboard** — Principal sees who teaches 30 periods vs 12 periods, balances automatically
3. **Leave Tracking** — Teacher applies leave in portal → writes to Sheet → principal approves → substitute auto-notified
4. **Payroll Ready** — Days present pull from attendance Sheet → salary calculates in Sheet → export for accountant

You control the Sheet structure. Scholar MS enforces who can write what.

This is where schools lose weeks.

**Old way:** 
Admin makes exam date sheet in Google Sheets. Shares with 20 teachers. Each teacher opens Sheet, finds their subject tab, enters marks. Someone sorts a column. Formulas break. VLOOKUP returns #REF. Principal waits 10 days for results.

**Scholar MS way:**

1. **Exam Setup** — Admin clicks “Create Exam” in portal. 2. **Teacher Marking Portal** — Teacher logs into Scholar MS on phone. Sees only “Grade 8 Math Mid-Term”. Simple table: Student Name | Marks. Enters marks, clicks submit.
3. <span class="text-[#1DA9CE] font-semibold">Marks write directly to the correct cell in Google Sheets. Teacher never opens the Sheet.</span> Cannot see other subjects. Cannot edit formulas. Cannot delete columns.
4. **Auto Report Cards** — Once all marks in, admin clicks “Generate”. System reads Sheet, creates PDF report cards for 500 students in 3 minutes. Emails to parents. 

**Result:** What took 2 weeks now takes 2 days. Zero formula errors. Zero “who deleted the Sheet” incidents.

Google Sheets does the heavy math. Scholar MS makes it foolproof.
